How we develop — decisions & why

The way two co-founders (+ Claude) ship MochiPOS without waiting on each other or risking the live booth. This is the "why" behind the rules; the board & roles live in team-guide.html.

1 · The one principle

Ship fast, don't wait on each other — make the merge gate a robot, not a person. We're two people heading to a real booth. A human approval queue would slow us without adding safety that automation can't already give. So: self-merge stays, and machines (CI, Claude, previews) catch problems before they reach production.

2 · The loop

branch→ open PR→ CI green + Claude /review + preview URL→ you click YOUR preview, self-merge→ production
  • Each PR gets a real, data-connected preview (own URL) — so you test your own change with data before merge, never on production, never waiting for the other person.
  • A broken build can't get there: the pre-push hook + CI block it.
  • Claude reviews every code PR automatically — your always-awake second pair of eyes, so neither of you is a bottleneck.

Each environment points at a different database, so a test can never hit real pilot data.
